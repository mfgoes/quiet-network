# Watchmakers by Quiet Network

## Overview

Watchmakers is a community-driven discovery platform for finding trusted watchmakers, repair shops, service centers, and watch-related recommendations.

The project is built on top of Quiet Network's existing infrastructure but is presented as a dedicated destination with a clear purpose:

**Find trusted watchmakers near you.**

Rather than launching another social platform, the goal is to solve a specific problem for watch enthusiasts first.

Recommended URL:

`/watchmakers`

The Quiet Network brand remains visible but secondary.

---

# Problem

Finding a trustworthy watchmaker is surprisingly difficult.

Questions enthusiasts frequently ask:

- Who services Rolex, Omega, Seiko, Tudor, and microbrands?
- Who works on vintage watches?
- Which shops are replica-friendly?
- Who offers pressure testing?
- What are typical service costs?
- How long is the turnaround?
- Which recommendations are still current?

Today this information is scattered across:

- Reddit
- Forums
- Discord servers
- Facebook groups
- Google Maps reviews

Most recommendations are difficult to verify, geographically search, or keep up to date.

---

# Vision

Create the easiest way to discover trusted watchmakers anywhere in the world.

Think:

**Google Maps + community knowledge + watch enthusiasts**

Instead of generic reviews, the platform focuses on information collectors actually care about.

# Core Experience

## Landing Page

**Find trusted watchmakers near you.**

Community reviews, service reports and recommendations from fellow enthusiasts.

- Explore map
- Search location
- Add watchmaker
- Submit service report

---

## Interactive Map

The map is the primary navigation method.

Users can:

- Search by city or country
- Browse nearby watchmakers
- Filter by services
- Discover community recommendations

Map pins represent watch-related businesses and locations.

Examples:

- Independent watchmakers
- Authorized service centers
- Vintage specialists
- Pressure testing services
- Trusted dealers
- Strap and accessory shops

---

## Watchmaker Profile Pages

Each watchmaker receives a dedicated page.

Information:

- Description
- Website
- Contact details
- Services offered
- Supported movements
- Community reviews
- Service reports
- Photos

---

## Structured Service Reports

The platform focuses on practical information rather than generic ratings.

Example:

- Watch: Clean GMT Master II
- Movement: DD3285
- Work: Regulation + pressure test
- Price: €40
- Turnaround: 2 days
- Would return: Yes

These reports become one of the platform's most valuable assets.

# MVP Scope

The first release focuses exclusively on watchmaker discovery.

Features:

- Interactive map
- Search
- Filters
- Watchmaker pages
- Reviews
- Structured service reports

Not included:

- Groups
- Events
- User profiles
- Messaging
- Following users

The goal is validation, not feature completeness.

---

# Success Metrics

Questions to validate:

- Will users submit watchmakers?
- Will users submit service reports?
- Will enthusiasts return after finding a watchmaker?
- Which filters are most used?
- Is replica-friendly support a meaningful differentiator?
